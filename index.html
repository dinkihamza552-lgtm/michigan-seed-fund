What the bill does
Creates a state-funded grant program administered through MEDC
Awards $10,000–$25,000 to Michigan college students founding technology-based startups
Non-dilutive — students keep full ownership of their company
Open to all Michigan colleges including community colleges, not just 4-year universities
Annual showcase event to connect winners with Michigan investors and mentors
Who qualifies
Enrolled in at least 3 credits at a Michigan college
Owns 50%+ of the startup
Tech-enabled or innovative business model
Graduates no earlier than 180 days before award
Proposed funding
$1–2M initial state appropriation
20–40 student startups funded per year
Administered by MEDC with university advisory boards
Annual program review for effectiveness

Bottom line
Michigan's startup ecosystem is growing — Detroit ranked #2 globally for ecosystem growth and the state ranks #3 for in-state VC investment. But 40% of graduates still leave, and no legislation specifically targets student founders. The Michigan Student Entrepreneur Seed Fund closes that gap, modeled on Maryland's enacted 2024 law, and gives Michigan the answer to brain drain it's been missing.
